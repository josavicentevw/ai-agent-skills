## Meeting Structure (30 minutes)

### 1. Check-in (5 min)
*How are you doing personally and professionally?*

**Questions to ask:**
- "How are you feeling this week?"
- "What's on your mind?"
- "How's your workload?"
- "How's your work-life balance?"

**Red flags to watch for:**
- Burnout signs
- Disengagement
- Confusion about priorities
- Team friction

---

### 2. Team Member's Agenda (15 min)
*Let the team member drive the conversation*

**Common Topics:**

**üéØ Current Work**
- "What are you working on?"
- "Any blockers I can help with?"
- "Do you have the resources you need?"
- "Is the scope clear?"

**ü§ù Team Collaboration**
- "How's working with [teammate]?"
- "Any team dynamics to discuss?"
- "Feedback on recent team changes?"

**üìà Career Growth**
- "What skills do you want to develop?"
- "Any projects you'd like to work on?"
- "How can I support your growth?"

**üí° Ideas & Feedback**
- "What could we do better as a team?"
- "Any process improvements?"
- "Thoughts on recent decisions?"

**üö® Concerns**
- "Anything worrying you?"
- "Any conflicts to address?"
- "How can I help?"

---

### 3. Manager's Topics (7 min)
*Share feedback, updates, and guidance*

**Feedback (if any)**
```
Situation: [What happened]
Behavior: [What you observed]
Impact: [Effect on team/project]
Future: [What to continue/change]
```

**Examples:**
- "Great job on the API design review. Your questions helped us avoid a major issue."
- "I noticed you've been quiet in sprint planning. Is everything okay?"
- "Your PR reviews have been really thorough lately. The team appreciates it."

**Updates**
- Company/team changes
- New projects or initiatives
- Relevant decisions
- Recognition opportunities

---

### 4. Action Items & Close (3 min)

**Document:**
- [ ] Action items (who, what, when)
- [ ] Follow-ups needed
- [ ] Topics for next time
- [ ] Any commitments made

**Example Action Items:**
```
- [ ] @Manager: Schedule pairing session with Sarah (by Friday)
- [ ] @TeamMember: Share feedback on new deployment process (by next 1:1)
- [ ] @Manager: Research conference options for React training (by EOW)
```

**Close with:**
- "Is there anything else you want to discuss?"
- "Thank you for sharing. See you next week!"

### For Career Growth Focused

**Monthly Themes:**
- **Month 1**: Skill assessment and gap analysis
- **Month 2**: Learning plan and resources
- **Month 3**: Progress check and adjustments
- **Month 4**: New opportunities discussion

**Questions:**
- "What technical skills do you want to build?"
- "Are there leadership opportunities you're interested in?"
- "What projects would challenge you?"
- "How can I help you grow?"

---

### For Performance Issues

**Structure:**
```
1. Acknowledge the concern
2. Listen to their perspective
3. Share specific examples
4. Collaborate on improvement plan
5. Set clear expectations and timeline
6. Offer support and resources
7. Schedule follow-up
```

**Example:**
```
Manager: "I want to discuss the missed deadlines on the API project. 
          Can you help me understand what happened?"

[Listen actively]

Manager: "I understand there were some blockers. Let's work together 
          on a plan to get back on track. What do you need from me?"
```

---

### For High Performers

**Focus Areas:**
- Stretch assignments
- Mentorship opportunities
- Conference/training
- Promotion readiness
- Preventing burnout

**Questions:**
- "What would make your work more challenging?"
- "Interested in mentoring junior engineers?"
- "What's your next career step?"
- "How can I help you get there?"

## Best Practices

### Do's ‚úÖ

1. **Let them drive** - It's their meeting
2. **Listen more than talk** - 80/20 rule
3. **Take notes** - Shows you care, helps follow-up
4. **Be consistent** - Don't cancel frequently
5. **Follow through** - Complete action items
6. **Be present** - Close laptop, silence phone
7. **Ask open questions** - Not yes/no
8. **Share context** - Help them understand decisions
9. **Show vulnerability** - Build trust
10. **End positively** - Acknowledge their work

### Don'ts ‚ùå

1. **Don't dominate** - This isn't a status update
2. **Don't cancel** - Shows they're not a priority
3. **Don't multitask** - Be fully present
4. **Don't skip follow-ups** - Erodes trust
5. **Don't make it only about work** - They're human
6. **Don't surprise with feedback** - Give it real-time
7. **Don't solve everything** - Coach, don't fix
8. **Don't compare** - Focus on individual
9. **Don't rush** - Take the full time
10. **Don't forget to listen** - Really listen

## Example 1-on-1 Note

```markdown
# 1-on-1: Sarah Chen
Date: January 20, 2026

## Check-in
- Feeling good, but a bit overwhelmed with two projects
- Enjoyed last week's team event
- Working from home going well

## Sarah's Topics

### Current Work - Payment Service
- Almost done with Stripe integration
- Found a bug in error handling (creating ticket)
- Will be ready for review by Wednesday
- **Action**: @Sarah - Share design doc with team (by Wed)

### Team Collaboration
- Really enjoyed pairing with Mike on the database migration
- Suggests more pairing sessions for complex work
- **Action**: @Manager - Set up weekly pairing rotation (by Friday)

### Career Growth
- Wants to learn more about system design
- Interested in leading a project next quarter
- Considering attending React Conf in May
- **Action**: @Manager - Research conference budget (by next 1:1)
- **Action**: @Sarah - Complete System Design course on Educative (by end of month)

## Manager's Topics

### Feedback - Positive
- Great job on the API design review last week
- Your questions about edge cases were spot-on
- Team really values your attention to detail

### Update - Team Changes
- New hire starting next month (Backend Engineer)
- Sarah will be onboarding buddy
- Discussed expectations and time commitment

## Action Items
- [ ] @Sarah: Share payment service design doc (Jan 22)
- [ ] @Sarah: Complete System Design course (Jan 31)
- [ ] @Manager: Set up pairing rotation (Jan 24)
- [ ] @Manager: Research React Conf budget (Jan 27)
- [ ] @Manager: Send onboarding buddy guidelines (Jan 21)

## Next Time
- Review progress on System Design course
- Discuss potential project lead opportunities
- Conference decision

---
Next 1-on-1: January 27, 2026
```
