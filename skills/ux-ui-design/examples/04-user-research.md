**Interview Highlight #1: Sarah, 32, Marketing Manager**

> "I have the best intentions on Sunday to meal prep for the week, but then I look at recipes and they all need ingredients I don't have. By the time I figure out what to make, it's 6 PM and I just order Postmates."

**Key Quotes**:
- "I wish there was a way to just tell me what to make with what I already have"
- "Meal kits are too expensive and create so much waste"
- "I don't mind cooking, I just hate the planning part"

**Behaviors Observed**:
- Uses Pinterest for recipe inspiration but never follows through
- Grocery shops 2x/week because of poor planning
- Spends 15+ minutes deciding what to order for delivery
- Has 3 meal planning apps installed, uses none regularly

**Pain Points**:
1. Can't find recipes that match available ingredients
2. Recipe sites have too many ads and long stories
3. Doesn't know what's in the fridge until she looks
4. Meal prep seems overwhelming

---

**Interview Highlight #2: Marcus, 28, Software Developer**

> "I literally eat the same 5 meals every week because it's easy. I know I should eat more variety, but I don't have the mental energy to figure out new recipes after work."

**Key Quotes**:
- "I want a 'Netflix for meals' - just tell me what to eat"
- "If it takes more than 30 minutes, I'm not cooking"
- "I'd pay for an app that actually saves me time"

**Behaviors Observed**:
- Orders Blue Apron sporadically but it piles up
- Follows r/MealPrepSunday but never actually meal preps
- Has strong opinions on recipe app UX
- Uses Wirecutter for product recommendations, wants similar for meals

**Pain Points**:
1. Decision fatigue from too many choices
2. Recipe complexity (wants "beginner-friendly" indicator)
3. No easy way to scale recipes for one person
4. Doesn't know what's healthy vs. marketed as healthy

**Interview Highlight #3: Jessica & Tom, 35/37, Parents of 2**

2. Food waste from forgotten ingredients

4. No way to plan around busy weeknight schedules

### Survey Results (n=342)

**Demographics**:
- Age: 18-25 (12%), 26-35 (48%), 36-45 (31%), 46+ (9%)
- Gender: Female (58%), Male (39%), Non-binary (3%)
- Household: Single (32%), Couple (38%), Family with kids (30%)
- Income: <$50K (15%), $50-75K (28%), $75-100K (31%), $100K+ (26%)

**Current Behaviors**:

**Q: How often do you cook at home?**
- Every day: 18%
- 4-6 times/week: 35%
- 2-3 times/week: 31%
- Once a week or less: 16%

**Q: How often do you eat out or order delivery?**
- Every day: 9%
- 4-6 times/week: 23%
- 2-3 times/week: 41%
- Once a week or less: 27%

**Q: How much time do you spend on meal planning per week?**
- 0 minutes (don't plan): 34%
- 1-30 minutes: 41%
- 30-60 minutes: 19%
- 1+ hours: 6%

**Q: What stops you from cooking more often?** (Select all that apply)
1. Lack of time: 73%
2. Don't know what to make: 58%
3. Don't have ingredients: 51%
4. Grocery shopping is inconvenient: 44%
5. Cooking skills/confidence: 31%
6. Cleaning up afterwards: 29%
7. Too expensive: 18%

**Q: What features would you use in a meal planning app?** (Select all that apply)
1. Recipe suggestions based on my preferences: 78%
2. Automated grocery list: 74%
3. Recipes using ingredients I have: 71%
4. Meal prep guides: 64%
5. Integration with grocery delivery: 62%
6. Nutritional information: 58%
7. Family meal planning: 41% (but 78% among parents!)
8. Social features (share meals): 23%

**Q: How much would you pay for a meal planning app?**
- $0 (free only): 31%
- $1-5/month: 28%
- $6-10/month: 26%
- $11-15/month: 11%
- $15+/month: 4%

**Willingness to pay increases with**:
- Higher income (70% of $100K+ earners would pay $6+)
- Parents (58% would pay $6+ vs 35% of non-parents)
- Current meal kit users (64% would pay $10+)

**Statistical Insights**:
- âœ… Strong correlation between eating out frequency and lack of meal planning (r=0.71, p<0.001)
- âœ… Parents 2.3x more likely to want family sharing features
- âœ… Users who meal prep on weekends spend 3.2 hours less on cooking during the week
